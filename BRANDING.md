## 🪧 Kurzbeschreibung (App Store / GitHub / ~50 Wörter)

Playr is a clean, local music player built with Qt6 and C++. It reads your files, remembers your library, and plays — that's it. No internet connection. No sign-in. No background processes. Just fast, native playback of your MP3, WAV, and FLAC collection, exactly the way you left it.

## 📝 Mittellange Beschreibung (~120 Wörter)

Most music apps want your data, your subscription, and your attention.
Playr wants nothing — except to play your music.

Built with Qt6 and C++17, Playr is a native desktop music player that stays out of your way. Drop in your MP3s, WAVs, or FLACs, and your library is ready. Playr reads metadata automatically — title, artist, all of it — and remembers your collection across every session.

There's a clean sidebar for browsing, a minimal player bar at the bottom, and a progress slider that actually works. Volume control is right there. Nothing is hidden behind a paywall or a settings screen.

Playr is for people who have CDs they ripped in 2009, flac albums they spent good money on, and zero interest in yet another streaming service.

Your music. Played properly.

## 📖 Langbeschreibung (~250 Wörter)

Somewhere along the way, music players got complicated.

They need accounts. They want to recommend things. They stream, they sync, they analyze your listening habits. They update themselves while you're trying to work. Every few years they redesign the interface and your muscle memory starts over.

Playr is the opposite of that.

Playr is a local desktop music player — native, fast, and built with exactly one purpose: to play the music on your hard drive. It's written in C++ with Qt6, which means it starts in milliseconds, uses almost no memory, and runs without needing an internet connection or a background service.

When you open Playr, you see your library. You click a song. It plays. The artist and title appear at the bottom, pulled directly from the file's metadata. A progress bar shows where you are. A volume slider sits quietly on the right. That's the whole interface.

Your library is stored locally in a simple JSON file, so it persists between sessions — and because it's just a text file, you can edit it, back it up, or move it anywhere you like.

Playr supports MP3, WAV, and FLAC. You can add files one at a time, remove songs you no longer want, and keep your library exactly the way you like it. No cloud sync. No recommendations. No ads. No premium tier.

It's not trying to be Spotify. It's trying to be a great music player — the kind that respects your files, your time, and your attention.

Music, stripped back. That's Playr.
